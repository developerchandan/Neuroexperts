Faq's

How is India better than other countries for medical treatment?
The India is known for its world-class healthcare facilities. It is the home to some of the best healthcare professionals and doctors who have extensive experience in their respective fields of medicine. Every medical treatment in India is backed by the latest technology and state of the art infrastructure. In comparison with other countries, the treatment in India is cost-effective than most other countries. India has the finest medical expertise, top-quality patient care services all available at an affordable cost.
Can you help me with my visa?
Yes, Spine and Brain Healers can help you get your visa once you confirm your visit to our medical facility. On getting your confirmation, we will send a visa requisition letter to the Indian Embassy in your country and a marked copy of the same to you.
For the visa, we need the following from your end:
  • Patient’s name and contact details
  • Patient’s attendant’s name
  • Patient’s country of residence
  • Patient’s tentative date of arrival to India for treatment
Will you arrange a pick-up facility from the airport?
Yes, our international patient’s team will arrange a pick-up facility once your date and time of arrival are confirmed. One of our team members will stay in touch with you and help you throughout your treatment in India.
Will you arrange my accommodation?
Yes, our team will help in arranging suitable accommodation based on budget preferences. They will ensure that your accommodation is in the vicinity of the hospital. Other services such as doctor-on, cab services, emergency ambulance, etc can also be arranged as per the requirement.
What about my appointments with the doctor?
One of our international patients’ team members will coordinate between you and the doctor. All the appointments with the doctor as well as other services will be managed by our team members.
Which modes of payment do you accept?
You can either make a wire transaction from your account to the Spine and Brain Healers accounts or make a direct payment at the hospital via cash, card, or traveler’s cheque.
