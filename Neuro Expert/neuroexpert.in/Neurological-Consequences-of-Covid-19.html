Neurological Consequences of Covid 19

Vikas Gupta 2021-03-20

Covid 19 has emerged as the most extensive and deadly pandemic ever since Spanish Flu. By far, all we know about Covid 19 virus is that it is affecting lungs severely as most of its early symptoms are associated with the respiratory system. But recent studies show that Coronavirus can affect any part of the body, including heart, stomach, brain and nervous system. Reports suggest that 1 in 3 covid 19 patients experience nerve damage, strokes, cranial nerve palsy, brain fog and balance problems. As per the report, 0.9 percent to 23 percent of covid-19 infected people have developed stroke, either as an initial manifestation or later manifestation of COVID-19 as a multi-organ involvement. Any delay in taking treatment of neurological disorders, as strokes can be life-threatening. So, in this blog, I will be discussing the neurological consequences of Covid 19.

Loss of sense of smell and taste:
Many patients had complained of loss of sense of smell, i.e., anosmia and loss of sense of taste, i.e., ageusia as an initial presentation of covid 19. This manifestation can be because of coronavirus targeting the olfactory nerve. The patient may remain asymptomatic but the sense of taste and smell may be stopped. However, this manifestation is for a short duration, and the patient regains the sense of smell and taste as soon as they recover from coronavirus.

Brain stroke
It is one of the leading factors of death in India. Patients with comorbidities like diabetes, hypertension have shown the occurrence of brain stroke. It is possibly believed that the virus binds at the site where the drugs to lower the BP binds, therefore causing uncontrolled hypertension and stroke. Studies also suggest that youngsters are prone to brain stroke because of clotting in vessels of the brain.

Headache
This is one of the common manifestations of covid 19 viruses. Headache is present in 13% of patients of SARS-CoV-2 19. The headache can be because of low oxygen supply to the brain because of the lungs getting damaged, or the virus infecting the brain, causing inflammation in meninges.

Neuromuscular
Facial palsy has been reported as a manifestation of Covid 19, i.e., involvement of facial nerve, causing weakness in one side of the face. Muscle Pain, soreness and tiredness are also reported, which can be because of inflammation of muscle or damage of muscle because of the virus. Gullian Bare Syndrome (GBS) is a rapid progressive limb weakness and usually develops 7-24 days after respiratory disease.

Summary
Covid 19 pandemic has affected almost every country showing lungs to be the chief target area. However, patients have also indicated neurological disorders that have led to more death and complications. Identifying the covid 19 related neurological diseases is the first step towards treating the patients. It may be possible that you are asymptomatic with neuro disorder.
