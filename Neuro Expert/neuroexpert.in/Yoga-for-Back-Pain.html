Yoga for Back Pain

Vikas Gupta 2021-03-20

Back Pain is the most common problem people are facing during this pandemic. This can be due to sitting on an uncomfortable chair for hours and working on your computer system in awkward positions. Back pain causes discomfort and may disturb your professional life. So, getting a permanent cure is essential to maintain your day-to-day schedule. Most of the doctor suggests stretching out and yoga at the initial level for treating back pain. Yoga helps you stretch and strengthen muscles that support the back and spine. It helps in stabilizing the spine and bone. But studies also suggest that yoga can be the reason for sprain and aches among people aged 65 and above. So proper form and duration should be kept in mind when you are performing yoga.

Here I am suggesting Yoga For Back Pain

CAT-COW
It stretches erector spinae, rectus abdominis, triceps, serratus anterior, gluteus maximus. It is an easy and gentle back-end stretch. It helps to reduce your back pain and stretches your muscles and strengthen them as well.

DOWNWARD FACING YOGA
This yoga pose helps relieve back pain and cure sciatica. It helps to work out imbalances in the body and improves strength. It works on muscles like hamstrings, deltoids, gluteus maximus, triceps, quadriceps.

EXTENDED TRAINGLE
This classic standing posture may help alleviate backache, sciatica, and neck pain. It stretches your spine, hips, and groin and strengthens your shoulders, chest, and legs. It may also help relieve stress and anxiety.

SPHINX POSE
It helps in relieving stress and back pain. It strengthens the buttock and spine and stretches the shoulder, chest, and abdomen. Muscles worked are erector spine, gluteal muscles, pectoralis major, trapezius, latissimus dorsi.

COBRA POSE
This gentle backbend stretches your abdomen, chest, and shoulders. Practicing this pose strengthens your spine and may soothe sciatica. It may also help to relieve stress and fatigue that can accompany back pain.

The Bottom Line
Yoga is the best way to cure back and spine problems in an easy and accessible way. But yoga is not recommended for every age group. Challenging twists and turns should be avoided by the elderly. In case you are pregnant, you should take advice from your doctor and do less demanding yoga as instructed by them. If you are not into yoga, itâ€™s not the right time to start your session during your pregnancy. In case of severe back pain, consult your doctor before starting any new yoga or exercise. Remember that yoga is the best way to cure your lower back pain, but it must be in a proper manner and under proper guidance. Learn from your yoga trainer, YouTube, magazines, google your steps, and then practice regularly.
